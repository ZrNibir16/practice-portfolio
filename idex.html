CUET

Chittagong University of Enginnering and Technology

Former Bangladesh Institute of Technology, Chittagong, abbreviated as BIT Chittagong is presently Chittagong University of Engineering & Technology (CUET).This Institute was created out of Engineering College, Chittagong that was established in 1968.Through a Government ordinance in 1986, the college was converted into an Institute of Technology. In 2003 this institute of technology was converted into a public university. The honorable President of Bangladesh is the Chancellor of the University. To know more about CUET

KUET

Khulna University of Enginnering and Technology

Khulna University of Engineering & Technology has a background of historical significance. Although this institution has completed an era as university, its laying foundation began 49 years ago. This university was established in 1967 as ‘Khulna Engineering College’ under the University of Rajshahi. Khulna Engineering College were converted into autonomous Institutes named as Bangladesh Institute of Technology (BIT) from July 01, 1986.BIT, Khulna became Khulna University of Engineering & Technology with the other BITs as per act passed by the National Parliament effective from September 01, 2003.

Sheikh Kamal IT Business Incubator, CUET

Established : 2022

The country's first university based Incubator is established at the campus of Chittagong University of Engineering & Technology (CUET) Chittagong. The Business incubator will help to bridge the gap between industries and academia for fostering the meaningful research and innovation. A project has been taken to boost up the initiatives.The incubator is constructed on 4.7 acres on the Cuet campus involving Tk 113 crore. In the incubator, there is a high speed internet connection through BTCL. This unique project, implemented from July 2017 to June 2022, includes an oval incubation building up to 10-storey with 50,000 square feet space.

Sheikh Kamal IT Training Center, KUET

Established : 2017

Khulna Hi-Tech park will be established in Khulna University Of Engineering & Technology (KUET) campus. The objectives of the program are to expand ICT Industries with infrastructure development, training on ICT, make skilled manpower and creating employment opportunities for trained youths. Concerned sources said, the Hi-Tech park will be set by expending taka around 300 crores upon 8 acres of land in KUET campus with the financial aid of ADB and an Institution of India. The structural work of the park is likely to start within 3 next months which may be completed within 2018.
